People Power Monument

The EDSA People Power Revolution, which took place from February 22 to 25, 1986, was a peaceful uprising that led to the downfall of Ferdinand Marcos, the long-reigning dictator of the Philippines. The revolution was sparked by widespread discontent over the rigged presidential election in February 1986, where Marcos claimed victory over his main rival, Corazon Aquino, the widow of slain opposition leader Benigno Aquino Jr. Many believed the election was fraudulent, leading to massive protests and civil unrest.

What made the EDSA revolution remarkable was its peaceful nature. Millions of Filipinos, from all walks of life, gathered along Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) in Metro Manila, calling for Marcos to step down.
